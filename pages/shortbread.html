Shortbread

Shortbread is a fantastic treat all year round. Baked in homes all over the country, it's been a well loved treat for centuries. Popular in Scotland since the 12th century, this biscuit is often found around tables and under many a tree at Christmas time. I love them when they're still warm, fresh out of the oven, but you've got to be quick in our house!

Prep: 20 mins Cook: 15 mins Makes: 20 biscuits

Ingredients

  • 150g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 100g butter, chilled and cubed
  • 50g caster sugar, plus 1 tbsp for sprinkling

Method

  1. Heat the oven 170C/150C fan/gas 3. Put the flour, butter and sugar into a mixing bowl. Use your hands to combine the ingredients until the mixture looks like breadcrumbs, then squeeze until it comes together as a dough.
  2. On a lightly floured surface, use a rolling pin to roll out the dough to Â½ cm thick. Cut the dough into fingers and place on a lined baking tray. Use a fork to create imprints, then sprinkle with the remaining caster sugar.
  3. Chill the dough in the fridge for 20 mins, then bake for 15-20 mins until golden brown. Remove the shortbread fingers from the oven and leave to cool on the tray for 10 mins.
